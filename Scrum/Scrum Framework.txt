Scrum Framework

*Scrum is an agile framework used for developing, delivering, and sustaining complex products.
*Although it was initially designed for software development, it has found applications in various fields, including research, sales, marketing, and advanced technologies

1 Product Owner
* Represents the stakeholders and is responsible for maximizing the value of the product by managing and prioritizing the Product Backlog.

2 Product Backlog
*A prioritized list of features, enhancements, and fixes that represent the work to be done on the product.

3 Sprint Planning
*A collaborative meeting where the Scrum Team plans the work to be done during the upcoming Sprint.

4 Sprint Backlog
*A subset of the Product Backlog items selected for the Sprint, along with a plan for delivering the product increment.

5 Sprint
*A time-boxed iteration, usually two to four weeks, during which the Scrum Team works to deliver a potentially shippable product increment.

Key aspects of Sprints in Scrum

--Daily Stand-up >> A daily meeting where team members discuss their progress, plans for the day, and any impediments they are facing.
--Increment >> The sum of all the completed and done Product Backlog items at the end of a Sprint, meeting the team's Definition of Done.

6 Sprint Review
*A meeting at the end of each Sprint where the Scrum Team presents the completed work to stakeholders and receives feedback.

7 Sprint Retrospective
*A meeting at the end of each Sprint where the team reflects on their performance and identifies improvements for the next Sprint.

8 Increment
*The sum of all the completed & potentially shippable product backlog items at the end of Sprint.

9 Scrum Master
*A servant-leader responsible for facilitating Scrum events, removing impediments, and helping the team continuously improve.
